# Accord Tech Solutions - Brand Voice & Cold Email Messaging System

## Goal of This Brand Voice

The goal of this brand voice is to make every Accord Tech Solutions message sound consistent, professional, human, and reply-focused.

This guide helps the ATS email marketing agent understand how to write cold emails, follow-ups, re-engagement emails, reply responses, and campaign messaging in a way that matches the company's positioning.

The main purpose is to make sure ATS does not sound like a generic agency or spammy lead generation vendor. Instead, ATS should sound like a practical B2B growth partner that helps companies improve prospect data, outreach quality, CRM operations, and marketing execution.

This brand voice should help the agent:

- Write simple and natural cold emails
- Keep every message short, clear, and professional
- Focus on the prospect's pain point before introducing ATS
- Choose the right service angle for each campaign
- Avoid hype, spammy wording, and overpromising
- Use soft CTAs that increase reply chances
- Keep ATS messaging consistent across all campaigns
- Support campaign approval before launch
- Improve reply rate, positive replies, and booked meeting opportunities

Every message created for ATS should follow this goal:

**Sound human. Stay relevant. Focus on the prospect. Create replies.**

- Too clever

Before finalizing any message, ask:

1. Does this sound like a real person?
2. Is it easy to understand?
3. Is the value clear?
4. Is the CTA easy to answer?

# 11. Cold Email Philosophy

ATS cold emails should not feel like mass marketing.

They should feel like a simple, relevant business note.

## Main Goal

The goal of the first email is not to close a deal.

The goal is to create a reply.

## Cold Email Rule

Every cold email must answer:

1. Why this prospect?
2. Why this message?
3. Why now?
4. What problem are we pointing to?
5. What simple next step are we asking for?

If the email cannot answer these, it is not ready.

---

# 12. Cold Email Structure

## First Email Formula

Use this structure:

1. Simple opening related to the prospect
2. One specific problem or observation
3. One relevant ATS solution angle
4. Soft CTA

## Recommended Length

- First email: 60-110 words
- Follow-up email: 25-70 words
- Re-engagement email: 20-60 words

## Paragraph Rule

Use 2-4 short paragraphs.

Do not write large blocks of text.

# 14. Follow-Up Email Rules

Follow-ups should not repeat the same pitch.

Each follow-up should add one new useful angle.

## Follow-Up Rules

- Keep it short.
- Do not sound annoyed.
- Do not guilt the prospect.
- Do not say "just checking in."
- Do not push for a meeting too early.
- Add one reason to reply.
- Use a soft CTA.

# 17. CTA Guide

## CTA Principle

ATS CTAs should be easy to answer.

The best CTA gets a simple reply like:

- Yes
- Sure
- Send it
- Not now
- Who is this for?
- Tell me more

## Good CTA Examples

Use these:

- Should I send a simple example?
- Would it be useful if I shared the process?
- Open to seeing how this could work for {{CompanyName}}?
- Is this something your team is currently improving?
- Should I share a short checklist?
- Would this be relevant for your team?
- Is this handled internally right now?
- Should I send over a sample structure?
- Would a quick data-quality review be useful?
- Should I leave this for now?

## Avoid These CTAs

Do not use:

- Can we book a 30-minute call?
- Are you free tomorrow?
- What time works for you?
- Click here to schedule.
- Let me know your calendar availability.
- Are you the decision-maker?
- Can I get 15 minutes of your time?

## Meeting CTA Rule

Only ask for a meeting after the prospect shows interest.
